WFP - Yemen Food Security Update

December 2024

Food Security Overview (Nov 2024)

In November 2024, 61% of surveyed households in Yemen struggled to obtain adequate food. This rate is 1% lower than the previous month but 31% higher than the previous year.

  • Inadequate food consumption was reported by 64% in southern Yemen (IRG areas) and 59% in northern Yemen (SBA areas).
  • Severe food deprivation (poor food consumption) remained alarmingly high: 36% in IRG areas and 34% in SBA areas.
  • SBA areas saw a 4% month-on-month drop in poor food consumption and a 7% drop compared to August 2024 (before recent food distributions).

Key Drivers of Food Insecurity

  • Economic Challenges: Widespread economic difficulties across the country.
  • Funding Shortages: Insufficient funding for humanitarian aid.
  • Pause in Food Assistance: Suspension of aid in most SBA districts previously.
  • Limited Income Generation: Reported by 71% in the north (highest in 5 years) and 59% in the south.
  • Conflict: A major challenge, especially in Sa'dah, Ma'rib, Al Jawf, Shabwah, and Ibb.
  • Health Issues: Obstacles related to health, exacerbated by the ongoing cholera outbreak (high caseloads in Al Hodeidah, Hajjah, Amran, Dhamar).

Coping Strategies (Nov 2024)

  • Severe Food-Based Coping: Used by 55% of households in the north and 48% in the south.
  • Emergency Livelihood-Based Coping: Adopted by 27% in the north and 26% in the south (significant year-on-year increases).
  • Begging: Indicated by 9% in the north compared to 5% in the south.

Economic Factors

Minimum Food Basket (MFB) Cost

  • IRG Areas: MFB cost reached new record highs monthly in 2024. In Nov 2024, it was 22% higher year-on-year due to currency depreciation, rising fuel prices, and global food prices. Ma'rib and Ad Dali' saw the highest annual rise.
  • SBA Areas: MFB cost saw a 9% decline year-on-year due to strict price controls.

Currency Exchange Rate (End Nov 2024)

  • IRG Areas: Yemeni Riyal (YER) hit an all-time low of YER 2,054/USD, losing 26% value year-on-year and 72% over five years. Driven by low foreign reserves, reduced oil exports, and lower remittances.
  • SBA Areas: Riyal remained relatively stable at YER 533/USD (1% depreciation year-on-year) due to strict controls, but facing economic slowdown and liquidity concerns.

Fuel Imports & Prices

  • Imports (Jan-Nov 2024): Similar levels via Red Sea ports compared to 2023 (-1%), but a 9% decline via southern ports (Aden/Mukalla).
  • Concerns: Fuel stock levels in SBA areas due to damaged storage infrastructure at Al Hodeidah/Ras Issa ports. No fuel imports via Al Hodeidah since late July (except limited JET A1).
  • Prices (IRG Areas): Record high petrol prices (+9% year-on-year) and increased diesel prices (+7% year-on-year), despite lower global oil prices, driven by currency depreciation and import issues.
  • Prices (SBA Areas): WFP unable to collect price data since June 2024.

Food Imports

  • Volume (Jan-Nov 2024): Increased by 14% through both Red Sea and southern ports compared to 2023.
  • Availability vs Access: Essential food items were available, but access remained challenging due to declining household purchasing power.

Global Food Prices (FAO FFPI - Nov 2024)

  • Reached the highest level since April 2023 (6% rise year-on-year), but still 20% below the March 2022 peak.
  • Vegetable Oil Price Index saw a significant increase (+7% month-on-month, +32% year-on-year), reaching the highest level since July 2022.

WFP Response

  • IRG Areas: Due to funding shortages, WFP reduced targeted beneficiaries from 3.6 million to 2.8 million starting from the 4th food assistance cycle (early Dec 2024).
  • SBA Areas: The new Targeted Emergency Food Assistance (TEFA) programme started in Nov 2024, assisting 1.4 million people with reduced rations.
  • TEFA Impact: Post-distribution monitoring showed improvement; the share of beneficiaries with poor food consumption dropped from 52% (Aug) to 37% (Nov), similar to levels before the assistance pause. WFP plans to expand TEFA.
