Syria Monthly Market Price Bulletin

January 2025 - Issue 121

Highlights

  • Cash liquidity remains a significant challenge, affecting finances and business. E-payment suspensions hinder electronic transactions, leading to long ATM queues for limited cash.
  • Official exchange rate averaged SYP 13,065/USD; parallel market rate was SYP 11,678/USD. Banks restrict currency exchange due to poor liquidity.
  • Public sector structural reform aims to reduce workforce from 1.3M to 900K. Major delays in salary and pension disbursements reported.
  • Minimum Expenditure Basket (MEB) fell 15% to SYP 2.5 million (from SYP 3M). Limited cash access restricts purchasing power.
  • Gas prices rose 23% year-on-year. Removal of domestic gas subsidies impacts low-income households.
  • Subsidized bread price effectively increased eightfold year-on-year (accounting for weight change), burdening food-insecure households.

Economic Updates

Customs Tariffs

  • New unified customs tariff system introduced in Syrian Transitional Government (STG) areas, causing significant price hikes (over 5x for some items) in Idlib and NW Aleppo.
  • STG justifies reform for budget shortfalls and protecting local industry, citing previous inconsistencies.
  • STG considering tariff reductions on essentials (fuel, flour) and establishing monitoring committees.
  • "Al-damima" fee removed.
  • Removal of military checkpoints lowered food transport costs.

Salaries and Pensions

  • STG aims to reduce government workforce from 1.3M to 900K.
  • Plans to increase salaries are pending government review.
  • Most retirees haven't received pensions due to difficulties obtaining proof-of-life certificates.
  • Central Bank lifted transaction restrictions (previously SYP 5M cap) for financial transfer companies.

Daily Wage (Unskilled Labor)

  • National daily wage unchanged, but declined in NWS (due to SYP/TL exchange rate fluctuations, stable in TL at ~100 TL).
  • Highest average wages in Coastal regions (SYP 50,250), lowest in Northeastern regions (SYP 34,250).

Global Food Prices and Inflation Trends (FAO)

  • January 2025 FAO Food Price Index (FFPI) averaged 124.9 points, down 1.6% from December, but 6.2% higher than Jan 2024.
  • Declines in sugar, vegetable oils, meat offset increases in dairy, cereals.
  • Cereal Price Index up slightly but below last year. Wheat steady, Maize rose, Rice fell 4.7%.

Minimum Expenditure Basket (MEB)

In January 2025, the national average MEB fell by nearly 15% from December to SYP 2,520,905 for a family of five (lowest in a year). This is 3% lower than Jan 2024 but 116% higher than two years ago.

The monthly minimum wage (SYP 278,910) covers only 11% of the MEB (18% of the food component).

Energy Prices

Fuel priced in USD but sold in SYP based on parallel exchange rate, causing price disparities.

  • Transport Diesel (Parallel Market): Avg SYP 11,879/L (-26% MoM, -3% YoY).
  • Butane Gas Cylinder (25L, Parallel Market): Avg SYP 267,169 (-24% MoM, +23% YoY). Highest YoY increase in Northeast (52%).
  • Removal of subsidized domestic gas impacts low-income households.
[Chart 7: Monthly Prices of Transport Diesel, SYP/L (January 2024-2025) would be displayed here.]

Exchange Rate (SYP/USD)

  • Official Rate: Decreased to SYP 13,065/USD (from 13,567). Liquidity shortages persist.
  • Parallel Market Rate: Averaged SYP 11,678/USD (lowest since Aug 2023). Appreciated 27% YoY, but depreciated 44% over two years.
  • Syria's reliance on food imports makes it vulnerable to currency fluctuations.
