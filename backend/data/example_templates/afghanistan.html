United Nations World Food Programme

AFGHANISTAN

Weekly Market Report

Issue 242: Week 1 - April 2025

Highlights

Food Items: In the first week of April 2025, the prices of main food commodities show a slight week-on-week decline. Compared to the same time last year, an overall decline is observed except for cooking oil and salt, which are significantly higher. Prices have remained relatively stable during 2025 due to a stable exchange rate. However, current food prices are still significantly higher compared to pre-Covid levels and June 2021, except for wheat grain, wheat flour, and cooking oil.

WFP Food Basket: Declined by 0.5% compared to last week, maintaining a stable trend in 2025. Year-on-year, the price is relatively unchanged (-2%). It remains 9% higher than the pre-Covid period.

Vegetable Items: Tomato price declined by 2.4%, while potato and onion prices increased by 1.7% and 9.3%, respectively. Prices are expected to decline with local production availability. Notable price variations exist across provinces due to local harvest and imports (mainly Iran and Pakistan).

Non-food Items: Minimal changes in DAP, urea, improved seeds, and animal feed prices compared to last week. Year-on-year trends show a slight decline for DAP, urea, and improved seeds. Urea and DAP prices remain significantly higher than June 2021, while improved seeds decreased.

Diesel: National average price is AFN 58.0/litre, down 0.7% from last week and 5% from last year. It is 29% higher than pre-Covid levels and 17% above June 2021 prices. Global average diesel price is $1.18/litre.

Labour Market: Slight improvement in labour availability as spring begins (construction/agriculture demand increases). However, workdays per week are significantly lower compared to pre-Covid and June 2021 levels, reflecting reduced opportunities.

Data Visualizations (Summary)

WFP Food Basket Prices: The report includes a graph showing the trend of WFP Food Basket prices in AFN and USD from 2020 to April 2025. Recent prices show relative stability in 2025 after significant fluctuations in previous years.

Casual Labour Wage/Wheat Flour ToT: A graph illustrates Nominal and Real Terms of Trade (ToT) for casual labour wage against low-quality wheat flour. Nominal ToT is 11.9 kgs, while Real ToT (adjusted for work availability) is 3.9 kgs, showing slight recent improvement.

National Average Price Trends: Graphs depict trends for Exchange Rate, Diesel, Fertilizers, Seeds, Animal Feed, Wheat, Rice, Pulses, Cooking Oil, Tomato, Potato, and Onion from 2020 to April 2025.

Notes

  • Price data collected weekly from 34 provincial capitals.
  • Rice types reported: Palawi (long-grain, imported, expensive) and Sholae (short-grain, domestic, cheaper).
  • Pulses price is an average of lentils, chickpeas, and red beans.
  • Pastoralist ToT: Kg of wheat grain obtainable by selling a 1-year-old female sheep.
  • Real Unskilled Labour ToT: Kg of wheat grain obtainable with average daily wage adjusted by average weekly workdays. Nominal ToT excludes work availability adjustment.
  • Casual Labour: Unskilled, hired short-term, lower pay rate.
  • Price Table Interpretation: Positive changes mean price decrease (good for consumers), negative changes mean price increase. For ToT/Wages, positive means improvement, negative means decline. Color coding (not implemented here) indicates significance of changes.
